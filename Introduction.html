Introduction

What is Amigurumi?

Amigurumi is a Japanese term for crocheting cute stuffed toys.

What is crocheting?

It is a needlework technique performed using a crochet hook and yarn.

Basic Crochet Techniques

Slip Knot

1. Make a loop and hook another loop through it

2. Tighten gently and slide knot up to hook

Chain Stitch (CH)

1. Yarn over hook and draw yarn through to form a new loop without tightening previous one

2. Repeat to form as many chain stitches as required. Do not count slip knot as a chain stitch.

Single Crochet (SC)

1. Insert hook into work (second chain from the hook on the starting chain)

- yarn over hook, and draw yarn through work only

2. Yarn over hook again and draw yarn through both loops on the hook

3. Insert hook into next stitch, repeat from step 1 to make another SC

Half Double Crochet (HDC)

1. Yarn over the hook and insert the hook into the work (third chain from the hook on the starting chain)

2. Yarn over the hook and draw up a loop — three loops on the hook

3. Yarn over the hook again and draw through all three loops on the hook—one half double crochet made

4. Yarn over the hook, insert the hook into the next stitch, repeat from step 1 to make another HDC

Double Crochet (DC)

1. Yarn over hook and insert hook into work (fourth chain from the hook on starting chain)

- Yarn over hook and draw through work only (three loops are on the hook)

2. Yarn over hook and draw through the first two loops only

3. Yarn over hook and draw through last two loops on the hook — one double crochet made

4. Yarn over hook, insert hook into next stitch, repeat from step 1 to make another DC
